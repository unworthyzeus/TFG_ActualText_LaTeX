General comments about the work progress
Project Objectives Reminder Before detailing the recent developments, it is important to briefly recall the primary goals of this project:
Develop a standalone deep learning software architecture capable of predicting key 6G FR3 channel parameters (delay spread, angular spread, and channel power) from 2D ground representations and varying UAV altitudes up to approximately 500 meters.
Accelerate channel generation by achieving a 10x to 100x reduction in computation time compared to traditional ray tracing simulations.
Attain high prediction accuracy against ground-truth data, targeting a Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) of approximately 3 to 5 dB for channel power, 50 ns for delay spread, and 20 degrees for angular spread. The second and third have been achieved easily (27ns and 10.7 degrees respectively)
Implement data augmentation pipelines and good heuristics to ensure the model outputs align with known deterministic formulas for 6G UAV-enabled networks.
Summary of Work Completed To date, the foundational research phase (WP1) has been successfully completed. This involved researching the formulas that apply to 6G UAV-enabled networks, identifying the best parameters for simulations, and reviewing the state-of-the-art in environment-aware Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). Following this, the project advanced into the initial stages of WP2, focusing on computing deterministic heuristics to establish a baseline prior (Stage 0). However, as the data simulation and modeling progressed, several technical challenges emerged that required an empirical reevaluation of the deep learning pipeline.
Incidences
During the execution of the project, several significant technical issues have been encountered that have diverted the initial course of the predictive model:
High error in the nLoS Prior: It has been detected that the prior in non-Line-of-Sight (nLoS) situations presents an unacceptably high error, sitting at around 40 dBs. This margin of error compromises the viability of the base model and has required urgent intervention for its improvement.
Ineffectiveness of GAN networks for high frequencies: Initially, the use of cGANs (Conditional Generative Adversarial Networks) was proposed for data simulation and augmentation in WP2. However, empirical tests have shown that GAN architectures do not help improve precision in the high-frequency components (high-frequencies in the image processing sense, of things that change quickly, have a high laplacian) of the channel maps.
Suboptimal current metrics and presence of outliers: Despite multiple attempts, the current model presents an RMSE of 17 dBs and an MAE of 6 dBs. Although there is an improvement compared to the initial heuristic prior (which has an overall error of 22.5 dBs), the metrics are still far from the target of 3-5 dBs established in the proposal. Furthermore, the model continues to suffer from the presence of significant outliers that distort the predictions.
Complexity of generalizable models (State of the Art deviation): According to the current State of the Art (SoA), developing a single model capable of generalizing across diverse city topologies while simultaneously handling vastly different antenna heights is highly unusual. Most existing literature relies on Reinforcement Learning (RL) tailored to specific cities, and grouping extremely diverse antenna elevations within a single model represents a rare and highly complex approach that has proven more challenging than initially anticipated.
Work Plan modifications
As a consequence of the described incidences, the development has not followed the original planning of the Work Packages (WPs) in a linear fashion. Instead of advancing sequentially, an empirical and exploratory approach ("trial and error") has been adopted, merging and altering the tasks of WP2 and WP3.
The main modification is the abandonment of the use of cGANs in favor of the design of a highly coupled sequential pipeline comprising a foundational heuristic stage and 3 deep learning stages:
Stage 0 (The Prior / Heuristics): Computation of deterministic heuristics to establish a baseline prior. Currently, this stage yields an overall initial error of 22.5 dBs. This heuristic prior serves as the fundamental input for the subsequent deep learning stages.
Stage 1: Use of a PMNet to calculate the residual with respect to the prior generated in Stage 0.
Stage 2: Implementation of a Tail Refiner that learns directly from the features extracted in Stage 1.
Stage 3: Development of a model dedicated exclusively to mitigating the error in nLoS, which learns from Stage 2. Additionally, a feedback loop has been introduced where Stage 1 learns from Stage 3 (if the latter exists and has been processed), creating an iterative improvement cycle.
Fallback Strategy: If further refinement of this unified pipeline proves unsuccessful in reaching the desired precision metrics, a fallback strategy will be implemented. This strategy involves dividing the problem space: for each type of city, the deep learning pipeline will be split into 9 specialized models (the 3 learning stages x 3 antenna height ranges: low antenna, mid antenna, and high antenna). This will drastically reduce the variance each individual model needs to learn.
This architectural change requires extending the dates of WP3 and overlapping the validation phases (WP4) to continuously test each of the stages of the new pipeline.
